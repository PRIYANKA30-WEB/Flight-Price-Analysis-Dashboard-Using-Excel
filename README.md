# Flight-Price-Analysis-Dashboard-Using-Excel
 Project Overview:
An executive-grade, fully interactive Excel dashboard analyzing 3,00,153 Indian domestic flight records across 6 airlines, 30 routes, and multiple pricing dimensions. Built to uncover hidden cost-saving patterns for travelers and pricing intelligence for airlines.

Key Findings:
-37% price surge for last-minute bookings — flights cost ₹19,300 avg when booked 1+ month ahead vs ₹26,500 in the final week
  Non-stop flights are the cheapest (₹9,400 avg) — counter-intuitive, as budget carriers dominate direct routes; 1-stop flights average       ₹22,900
-Late Night is the cheapest departure slot at ₹9,300 avg — 60% cheaper than Night flights (₹23,100)
-AirAsia offers 86% savings vs Vistara (₹4,100 vs ₹30,400) — the widest fare gap in the dataset
-Vistara commands 42.6% market share yet charges a 7.4× premium — single-carrier dominance signals pricing power on key routes

 How to Use:

1.Download Flight_Price_Analysis_Dashboard.xlsx
2.Open in Excel 2016 or Microsoft 365 (required for slicers)
3.Use the slicers on the right to filter by Airline, Time, Stops, or Booking Window
4.All charts update dynamically based on your selection
5.Read the Insights & Recommendations section at the bottom for actionable tips


 Business Insights:
For Customers (Cost-Saving Tips)

-Book at least 1 month in advance to avoid the 37% last-week price premium
-Choose Late Night departures — consistently the cheapest slot at ₹9,300 avg
-Fly non-stop — direct flights are paradoxically cheaper (budget airlines operate them)
-Use AirAsia or IndiGo for budget travel — up to 86% cheaper than Vistara

For Airlines (Pricing Intelligence)

.Vistara has monopolistic tendencies — premium pricing + 42.6% volume dominance
.SpiceJet has the fewest flights (3% share) — underutilized route capacity
.Demand surge in the final booking week is an opportunity for dynamic pricing optimization
.Chennai-Bangalore route has highest avg price (₹25,100) — potential for budget carrier entry
